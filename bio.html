Dr. Cameron B. Chiarot

Health Economist | Epidemiologist | Biostatistician

Dr. Cameron B. Chiarot is a health economist, epidemiologist, and biostatistician who specializes in health system resilience within global health contexts characterized by resource limitations. His research is primarily conducted in Zambia, where he employs advanced statistical analysis and disease modelling techniques to assess the impact of adverse interactions between macroeconomic shocks and chronic health system stressors.

Dr. Chiarot holds a PhD from the University of Waterloo's School of Public Health Sciences, an MBA with Distinction from York University’s Schulich School of Business, an Honors Bachelor of Science from the University of Western Ontario, and a certificate in Healthcare Executive Leadership from the University of Toronto’s Rotman School of Management. He has been nominated for the Governor General’s Gold Medal in recognition of his outstanding academic performance and distinguished scholarly record.
